<p><p>The Arrayed Waveguide Grating (AWG) multiplexers market is primarily divided into two types: thermal AWGs and athermal AWGs. Thermal AWGs rely on temperature control for wavelength stability, making them suitable for environments with varying temperatures. In contrast, athermal AWGs are designed to maintain performance across a broader temperature range without requiring heating or cooling, ensuring consistent operation. These multiplexers are crucial in telecommunications and data center applications for efficient signal processing and wavelength division multiplexing.</p></p>
